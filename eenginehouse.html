E Engine House

The E engine house was built in the 1920's to contain a large twin impeller pump dpowered by an electric motor. However in 1980s the pump was taken off and the building was destroyed

The transferable engine used to work on the farm, but was restored by an electric motor. Eventually it was brought back to the station where it was attached to the position steam supply and the movement resumed to drive lime apparatus

The agitator engine shifted paddles scaled on a long shaft below in the sewage culvert.

The centrifugal storm water pump was driven by this engine. Other than the beam engines, this is the extensive other engine at the station and it is the only other engine made with a condenser.

For more information, please contact our Education team.
